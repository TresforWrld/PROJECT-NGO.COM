2026 Programs

Five Programmes. One Mission.

From GBV prevention to child safeguarding, women's leadership to civic activism — our 2026 programme calendar brings communities together around what matters most.

Program 01
GBV Prevention

Initiate

18–19 April 2026
Uganda

Initiate is an annual conference hosted in Uganda focused on gender-based violence prevention, survivor-centred support systems, and strengthening community protection mechanisms. It convenes civil society organisations, local leaders, service providers, and policymakers to discuss practical approaches to prevention, response, and long-term social change.

GBV Prevention & Awareness Survivor Referral Pathways Policy Engagement Community Mobilization
Program 02
Civic Activism

The Justice Network

13–14 June 2026
Burundi

The Justice Network is hosted in Burundi and focuses on activism, civic participation, and human rights advocacy. The conference serves as a platform for activists, youth leaders, journalists, and civil society actors to exchange ideas on strengthening citizen engagement, promoting accountability, and advancing social justice movements.

Grassroots Activism Human Rights Advocacy Civic Engagement Campaign Strategy
Program 03
Women's Leadership

Her Voice Summit

25–26 July 2026
Zambia

Her Voice Summit centres on women's leadership, empowerment, and inclusive participation in decision-making spaces. It highlights women-led innovation, leadership development, and economic empowerment while creating mentorship opportunities for emerging leaders across the region.

Women in Leadership Economic Empowerment Entrepreneurship Mentorship & Representation
Program 04
Child Protection

Safe Futures Forum

12–13 September 2026
Zambia

Safe Futures Forum focuses on child protection, safeguarding, and adolescent wellbeing. It brings together education actors, child rights advocates, social workers, and community leaders to strengthen systems that protect children and young people from harm, exploitation, and unsafe environments.

Child Safeguarding Adolescent Wellbeing Prevention of Abuse Safe Learning Environments
Program 05
Community Development

Community Resilience Exchange

7–8 November 2026
Uganda

Community Resilience Exchange focuses on community development, resilience-building, and sustainable local solutions. It promotes collaboration among practitioners working on livelihoods, local innovation, and resilience in vulnerable communities facing economic and environmental pressures.

Community Resilience Sustainable Livelihoods Local Development Innovation Climate & Social Adaptation
Impact

What Our Programs Deliver

Every programme is designed to produce tangible outcomes — not just conversations, but commitments, networks, and action plans that outlast the gathering itself.

Shared Learning
Participants gain new insights, best practices, and innovative approaches from peers working in similar contexts across the continent.
Network Building
Gatherings strengthen relationships between partners, activists, and faith leaders, creating lasting networks for collaboration and mutual support.
Collective Strategy
Collaborative planning sessions produce aligned strategies, joint advocacy positions, and coordinated responses to emerging challenges in each context.
Survivor Visibility
Programmes centre survivor voices and lived experience, ensuring those most affected by injustice shape the solutions designed to address it.
Faith-Grounded Action
Every programme anchors its work in faith values — dignity, justice, and compassion — mobilising churches and faith communities as agents of change.
Cross-Border Connection
Delegates from multiple countries exchange knowledge and build cross-border solidarity, amplifying impact beyond any single national context.
