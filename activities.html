What We Do

Core Activities of
HGY Mission

A comprehensive, faith-centred framework of 15 activities through which we protect human dignity, support survivors, and build justice-conscious communities across Africa.

01
Survivor
GBV Response

Safe Space for GBV Survivors

Provide a confidential, trusted environment where survivors of Gender-Based Violence can safely share experiences, receive emotional support, and access immediate guidance without fear of stigma or retaliation.

02
Survivor
Healing

Psychosocial Support and Trauma Healing

Offer trauma-informed counselling, psychosocial first aid, and emotional healing sessions for survivors, human rights defenders, and vulnerable individuals affected by violence, threats, or displacement.

03
Survivor
Long-Term Support

Mentorship and Long-Term Follow-Up

Establish mentoring programmes that accompany survivors and defenders through recovery, helping them rebuild confidence, strengthen decision-making, and access continued support over time.

04
Emergency
Crisis Response

Emergency Relocation for Harassed Human Rights Defenders

Coordinate urgent temporary relocation for threatened defenders through church networks, faith communities, and trusted protection partners to ensure immediate safety and continuity of protection.

05
Faith
Church Partnership

Church-Based Protection Partnerships

Mobilise local churches and faith institutions as community protection hubs that provide shelter, accompaniment, referrals, and moral support to at-risk individuals and families.

06
Legal
Rights Awareness

Legal Referral and Rights Awareness

Link survivors and defenders to legal aid providers, document abuses, and educate communities on rights, reporting mechanisms, and available protection systems.

07
Faith
Pastoral Care

Spiritual Care and Faith-Based Healing

Provide pastoral counselling, prayer support, spiritual accompaniment, and faith-sensitive healing approaches for individuals whose trauma intersects with spiritual distress.

08
Community
Prevention

Community Dialogue and Prevention Programmes

Facilitate discussions with families, religious leaders, youth, and community actors to prevent violence, challenge harmful norms, and promote dignity, equality, and nonviolence.

09
Training
Capacity Building

Capacity Building for Human Rights Defenders

Train defenders in personal security, documentation, digital safety, stress management, and resilience to strengthen their protection capacity and long-term effectiveness.

10
Survivor
Reintegration

Livelihood and Reintegration Support

Help survivors regain stability through skills training, small livelihood support, and reintegration pathways that reduce vulnerability and restore independence after crisis.

11
Emergency
Referral Network

Referral Network for Medical and Shelter Services

Maintain rapid referral systems for medical care, temporary shelter, legal support, and emergency response for urgent protection cases requiring immediate intervention.

12
Legal
Documentation

Advocacy and Documentation

Document cases, monitor violations, and advocate for accountability while protecting survivor confidentiality and dignity at every stage of the process.

13
Community
Reconciliation

Peacebuilding and Social Cohesion

Use faith-based dialogue to reduce tensions, rebuild trust, and support reconciliation in communities affected by conflict or violence, fostering lasting social healing.

14
Legal
Case Management

Confidential Case Management

Develop secure systems for handling sensitive survivor and defender information, ensuring confidentiality, ethical follow-up, and protection from further harm throughout the process.

15
Emergency
Crisis Intervention

Rapid Response for Crisis Situations

Maintain emergency mechanisms for urgent transport, safe referrals, crisis counselling, and immediate intervention when threats escalate — available when every moment counts.
